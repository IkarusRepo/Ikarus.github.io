\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
\subsubsection*{template$<$class Material\+Impl$>$\newline
struct Ikarus\+::\+Material$<$ Material\+Impl $>$}
Consider a hyper elastic material with the free Helmholtz energy \[\require{cases}\psi: \begin{cases}\mathbb{R}^{3\times 3} &\rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ \BC
&\mapsto \psi(\BC) \end{cases}.\]

Then, the value of this potential energy is return by \mbox{\hyperlink{a01304_a297b4cab80330a8c473dbc06552c8808}{stored\+Energy }} and its first derivative (the stresses) by \mbox{\hyperlink{a01304_af10f8631c73d1c4cb78f8b6d44bf6f1a}{stresses }} and the second derivatives (the tangent moduli) by \mbox{\hyperlink{a01304_a00e7bb30244df751617e1e613d382ea0}{tangent\+Moduli }}.

The passed strains can be in several formats, i.\+e., $\BC$ can be the \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finite_strain_theory\#Cauchy_strain_tensor_(right_Cauchy\%E2\%80\%93Green_deformation_tensor)}{\texttt{ right Cauchy-\/\+Green tensor}}, the \href{https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finite_strain_theory\#Deformation_gradient_tensor}{\texttt{ deformation gradient}} $\mathbf{F}$ or linear strains. The current supported tags are given by \mbox{\hyperlink{a00305_gacef1e451de1a99845c59d7391064fb8f}{Ikarus\+::\+Strain\+Tags}}.
